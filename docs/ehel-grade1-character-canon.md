## Why this canon (occurrence counts that drove it)

Counted by name token in the 36 passages, before any edit.

- **Ishmael** — 23 tokens. big brother 7 (u4×3, u5×1, u8×3, plus explicit "her big brother" in all three) vs baby brother 5 (u2) vs four-year-old 7 (u3) vs market man 2 (u6) vs school friend 2 (u9). Explicit relationship label "(her) big brother" appears in **4 units** (3 after u3's "big brother Adam" is resolved to him) against one unit each for every other role → **big brother wins**.
- **Samira** — 12 tokens. friend/peer girl 9 (u3×7 "her friend", u7×2 "a girl … going to school") vs little sister 2 (u4, u8) vs grandmother 1 (u9) → **friend wins** (9 > 2 > 1), which also removes the sharpest clash, "Amal's grandmother, Samira".
- **Adam** — 10 tokens. boy/classmate 3 (u1×2 "Do you want to be my friend?", u7×1 boy on a bicycle) across 2 units vs big brother 5 (u3, 1 unit) vs grown shopkeeper 2 (u9, 1 unit) → **boy classmate wins on unit count**, and u3's "big brother Adam" is re-pointed at Ishmael, which fixes defect 5 for free (no new name needed for that role).
- **Grandmothers** — only two are *named*: Selma 4 tokens (u6) vs Samira 1 token (u9) → **Selma wins**. "Grandma" (u2) and "Grandmother" (u7) are unnamed and cost nothing; *Ayeeyo* (u4, u5) is a term of address, not a rival name. Because u9 also had a doctor called Selma, the **doctor** was renamed (1 token) rather than the grandmother (4 tokens).
