# Alphabet Unit Explainer Script

Welcome to the Alphabet Unit.

In this unit, we will learn the Arabic letters step by step. We will listen, watch, repeat, speak, match, and write. Take your time. Do your best. Every step helps you get closer to reading the Quran.

## Step 1: Lecture

First, you will watch the lesson.

This step helps you understand what you are going to learn.

You will hear the teacher explain the Arabic alphabet and why the letters are important.

Watch carefully. Sit in a quiet place. Keep your eyes on the lesson and listen with focus.

## Step 2: Rules

Now you will learn the alphabet rules.

This step helps you understand the letters before you start practicing.

You will learn that the Arabic alphabet has 29 letters. You will learn letter names, letter sounds, dots, heavy letters, light letters, and short vowels.

Read carefully. When you are ready, press Rules to play the rules audio. Listen all the way to the end two times. After the second pass, the step is complete.

## Step 3: Listen

Now it is time to listen.

This step helps your ears learn the sound of each letter.

You will hear every letter in the alphabet. You will hear the letter name and the letter sound.

Do not repeat yet. Just listen carefully. Try to notice how each letter sounds different.

## Step 4: Watch

Now you will watch the letters.

This step helps your eyes see how each letter is said and formed.

You will watch videos for the letters.

Look carefully at the screen. Watch the mouth, the sound, and the letter. You will get your turn to repeat soon.

## Step 5: Phonetics

Now you will learn how to say the letters correctly.

This step helps you know where the sound comes from.

Some sounds come from the lips. Some sounds come from the tongue. Some sounds come from the throat.

Listen to the explainer first. Then watch and practice the sound in your mind. This helps you say the letters clearly.

## Step 6: Repeat

Now it is your turn to repeat.

This step helps your mouth practice the letters.

You will hear the teacher say a letter. Then you will repeat after the teacher.

Say the letter clearly. Do not rush. Try again if you need to. Practice makes your voice stronger.

## Step 7: Letter Clue

Now you will use clues to remember the letters.

This step helps you connect each letter with something easy to remember.

You may see a picture and hear a clue sound for each letter.

Look at the picture. Listen to the clue. Say the letter in your mind and remember it.

## Step 8: Speak

Now you will speak the letters.

This step helps you check your own pronunciation.

You will listen to the correct sound, then record your own voice.

Say the letter clearly into the microphone. Listen to yourself. If it sounds good, continue. If not, try again.

## Step 9: Match

Now you will play a matching game.

This step helps you know which letter matches the sound.

You will hear a sound and choose the correct letter.

Listen carefully before you choose. If you make a mistake, do not worry. Try again and keep learning.

## Step 10: Sound Clue

Now you will learn letter sounds with word clues.

This step helps you hear the letter inside a word.

You will see a letter, a word, and a picture. You will hear the sound and the word.

Listen to the letter. Listen to the word. Try to hear how the letter sound is used.

## Step 11: Animate

Now you will watch how the letters are written.

This step helps your eyes learn the writing movement.

You will see the letters being written on the screen.

Watch the direction. Watch where the letter starts and where it ends. This will help you when you write.

## Step 12: Write

Now it is time to write.

This step helps your hand practice the Arabic letters.

You will trace and write the letters using writing guides.

Follow the shape slowly. Stay on the lines. Try your best to make each letter clear and neat.

## Step 13: Submit

Now you will finish the unit.

This step helps you show your final work.

You will make a final recording so your teacher can hear your practice.

Speak clearly. Take your time. When you are done, submit your work.

## Final Message

Great job.

You have practiced the Arabic alphabet in many ways.

You listened with your ears.

You watched with your eyes.

You repeated with your mouth.

You matched with your mind.

You wrote with your hand.

Now you are one step closer to reading the Quran.

Keep practicing every day.
