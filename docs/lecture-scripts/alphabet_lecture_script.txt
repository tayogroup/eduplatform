Alphabet Unit Lecture Script

Purpose:
This script explains how a student should use the Alphabet Unit from start to finish.
Use it for the narrated lecture video and for any future voice recording.

Suggested voice:
Warm, friendly, clear, patient, child-friendly English.

Script:
Assalamu alaykum, and welcome to the Alphabet Unit.

In this unit, you will learn the Arabic letters step by step.

You will watch, read, listen, practice, speak, match, write, and submit your work.

Take your time. The goal is not to finish quickly. The goal is to learn clearly and beautifully.

Step 1 is Lecture.

The purpose of the Lecture step is to show you how the whole unit works before you begin.

When you are on this step, click the Lecture button.

Watch the video carefully. Listen to the teacher and keep your eyes on the screen.

This step is complete when the lecture video finishes. The unit will then move you to the Rules step.

Step 2 is Rules.

The purpose of the Rules step is to help you understand the alphabet rules before you practice.

In this step, you will learn that the Arabic alphabet has twenty-nine letters.

You will learn letter names, letter sounds, letter shapes, dots, heavy letters, light letters, and short vowels.

To play the Rules audio, click the Rules button.

The Rules audio will play twice, two passes.

Listen to and read the rules carefully.

This step is complete when the Rules audio has finished playing twice.

Step 3 is Listen.

The purpose of Listen is to train your ears.

In this step, you will hear the teacher say each letter.

Your job is to listen only. Do not repeat yet.

When you see the Listen step, click the Listen button to hear the letters one after another, or click a letter card, such as Alif.

For example, you may hear Alif, Ba, Ta, and Tha.

The card will glow while the sound is playing.

This step is complete when all the letters have been heard.

Step 4 is Watch.

The purpose of Watch is to help your eyes see how the letters are pronounced and formed.

When you see the Watch step, click the Watch button to watch the letters one after another, or click a letter card, such as Alif.

The system opens a teacher video for the letter.

Look carefully at the screen. Watch the teacher's mouth, the letter shape, and the way the sound is produced.

For example, when you watch Kha, notice that it is different from Ha and Jeem.

When the video finishes, close it or let the unit continue.

This step is complete when you have finished watching all the letter videos.

Step 5 is Phonetics.

The purpose of Phonetics is to help you say each letter from the correct place in your mouth.

Some sounds come from the lips, some from the tongue, and some from the throat.

When you reach this step, click the Phonetics button or click a letter card.

First, the system plays a short video of the teacher saying that letter.

Watch the teacher's mouth carefully.

Next, the articulation page opens.

This page shows how to make the letter sound.

The explainer audio starts automatically, so keep listening.

When the explainer audio finishes, the system plays the letter video again.

Watch one more time and notice how the teacher makes the sound.

After that, the articulation page returns with helpful buttons.

You can click Explainer to hear the teaching again, Play Letter to hear the letter sound, or Play Video to watch the teacher again.

When you are ready, click Next Letter.

For example, with Alif, the page reminds you that Alif is a silent letter.

Relax, open your mouth slightly, keep it open, and recognize the tall, straight letter.

This step is complete when you finish the required phonetics practice.

Step 6 is Repeat.

The purpose of Repeat is to help your mouth practice after your ears have listened.

When you see the Repeat step, click the Repeat button.

First, listen to the teacher. Then repeat the letter clearly.

The unit will show a small card that says, Your turn, repeat the letter.

Click Record, then say the letter clearly.

If the system asks for microphone permission, allow it.

Do not rush. Say one letter at a time.

For example, if the teacher says Alif, you say Alif after the teacher.

You are given three chances to pronounce each letter correctly.

This step is complete when you have repeated the required letters.

Step 7 is LetterClue.

The purpose of LetterClue is to help you remember the letter shapes and dots.

When you see the LetterClue step, click the LetterClue button.

The system opens a clue picture.

Look at the picture, listen to the clue sound, and connect the clue to the letter.

Pay close attention to the dots.

Dots help you tell Ba, Ta, and Tha apart.

This step is complete when you have finished the required letter clues.

Step 8 is Speak.

The purpose of Speak is to help you evaluate your own pronunciation.

Click a letter, listen to the teacher, and then record your voice.

First, click the microphone button if the unit asks for it.

Then click Record and say the letter.

After you record, listen to yourself.

If your pronunciation matches the teacher's, click Done or continue.

If it does not match yet, record again and keep practicing.

If the system asks for microphone permission, allow it.

This step is complete when you have finished the required speaking practice.

Step 9 is Match.

The purpose of Match is to check whether you know which sound belongs to which letter.

When you see the Match step, listen to the sound first.

Listen carefully, then choose the matching letter.

For example, if you hear Meem, choose the letter Meem.

If you make a mistake, stay calm, try again, and keep learning.

This step is complete when you have finished the matching game.

Step 10 is SoundClue.

The purpose of SoundClue is to help you hear a letter sound inside a word.

When you see the SoundClue step, click the SoundClue button.

The system opens a word picture.

You will see a letter, a word, and a picture.

Listen to the letter sound and then listen to the word.

For example, you may see Alif with Asad, which means lion.

This step is complete when you have finished the required sound clues.

Step 11 is Animate.

The purpose of Animate is to show you how letters are written before you write them yourself.

When you see the Animate step, click the Animate button.

The system opens an animated writing video.

Watch where the letter starts, the direction of the movement, and where the letter ends.

For example, notice how Ba is written and where the dot goes.

This step is complete when you have finished watching the required writing animations.

Step 12 is Write.

The purpose of Write is to help your hand practice writing the Arabic letters.

When you see the Write step, click Write or open the writing practice.

Trace the guide letter slowly, stay on the lines, and then try to write the letter neatly.

Use Reset if you want to try again.

Use Print if your teacher wants you to practice on paper.

This step is complete when you have finished the required writing practice.

Step 13 is Submit.

The purpose of Submit is to send your final work to your teacher.

Click Submit when you are ready.

First, use Listen or Watch if you would like one final practice session.

Then click Start Recording.

Say all the letters or words together in one clear recording.

When you finish, click Stop.

Review your work before you send it.

Then click Submit for Teacher.

This step is complete when your final recording or work has been submitted.

Remember these helpers.

Use Back if you need to return to the previous screen.

Use Step Back if you need to redo the previous step.

If a message appears, read it carefully before continuing.

Great job.

Listen with attention.

Watch with focus.

Repeat with care.

Write with patience.

You are ready to begin the Alphabet Unit.
